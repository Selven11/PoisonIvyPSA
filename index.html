Poison Ivy Awareness

Learn to identify, prevent, and treat poison ivy exposure. Your safety matters.

Description

Poison ivy is a plant native to North America, commonly found in forests, fields, and urban areas. It typically grows as a vine or shrub and is recognizable by its clusters of three pointed leaves.

  • Thrives in forests, fields, and urban areas
  • Produces an oily resin called urushiol, which triggers allergic reactions
  • Contact with the plant, its sap, or contaminated objects can cause exposure

Understanding Poison Ivy

Signs
Symptoms
Treatment

Initial Signs

  • Clusters of three pointed leaves
  • Reddish stem color in spring
  • Climbing vines or low shrubs
  • Green berries in summer
  • Red/orange leaves in fall

Common Symptoms

  • Redness and itching (12-48 hours after exposure)
  • Raised, blistering rash in streaks
  • Swelling and oozing blisters
  • Symptoms may last up to three weeks

Treatment Options

  • Immediate washing with soap and cool water
  • Apply calamine lotion or hydrocortisone cream
  • Take oral antihistamines
  • Use cool compresses or oatmeal baths
  • Seek medical attention for severe cases

Prevention Tips

Identification

  • Look for clusters of three pointed leaves
  • Watch for shiny leaves with smooth or jagged edges
  • Notice red/yellow fall coloring and white berries

Protection

  • Wear long sleeves and pants in risk areas
  • Use barrier creams like ivy block
  • Clean tools and clothing after potential exposure
